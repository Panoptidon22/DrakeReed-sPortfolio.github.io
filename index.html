Featured Case Study

Breathe Easy Berkshires

Breathe Easy Berkshires was a EPA-funded air quality monitoring and public health research project in Pittsfield, Massachusetts.

The project connected air quality monitoring data with public health and Environmental Justice indicators to better understand how pollution is experienced across Pittsfield neighborhoods. Drake's role included spatial analysis, interactive map design, public health research, community engagement, and environmental data visualization using ArcGIS and public datasets such as CDC PLACES.

The resulting interactive public map allows residents, researchers, and local organizations to explore air quality conditions alongside modeled health indicators, wildfire smoke impacts, and Environmental Justice geographies. Together, the project’s findings support future community-based air quality research, public communication strategies, and environmental justice advocacy in Pittsfield. Further project findings, maps, and analysis can be explored through the full Breathe Easy Berkshires EPA final report.

Featured Case Study

Climate Vulnerability, Impact, and Adaptation Analysis

The Climate Vulnerability, Impact, and Adaptation Analysis was an 18-month study developed to support climate planning and environmental justice work in New York City.

As part of the project management team at The Tishman Environment and Design Center, Drake worked alongside researchers, climate scientists, and planners to support climate vulnerability and adaptation research. In collaboration with the Mayor’s Office of Climate and Environmental Justice, interagency partners, and the New York City Panel on Climate Change, the project developed data-driven analyses examining future climate conditions and their social and economic impacts across the city.

The study explored four key areas: climate risk projections and compound or sequential hazards; extreme rainfall projections; the economic and public health impacts of climate change; and the development of a Flood Vulnerability Index. The VIA process was designed to support climate and environmental justice-centered planning by helping policymakers better understand future risks and vulnerabilities across New York City.

About

Drake is an interdisciplinary artist, geographer, and practitioner-scholar whose work investigates environmental justice (EJ) in frontline communities, as well as state sponsored violence. In his career as a EJ scholar, he blends data visualization and qualitative methods to develop climate science reports and participatory art on a community level. Through his art Drakes mission is to preserve the history of Black and oppressed people's liberation, protecting it from whitewashing, and sharing it as an accessible educational resource. His work is grounded in urban design and cartography, he uses mapping as a medium to archive resistance movements and critique state-sponsored surveillance mechanisms. He also experiments with illustration, photography, and printmaking to tell stories of both abolition and the evolution of settler colonial constructs.
